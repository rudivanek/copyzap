## 1. Primary Target Segment

**Company Type:**
- Small and mid-sized marketing agencies
- Website development agencies with copywriting service lines
- Copywriting agencies

**Company Size:**
- 2–30 employees

**Revenue Range:**
- **[ASSUMPTION]** $150,000–$2,000,000 ARR
- Agencies with enough client volume to justify structured copy workflows, but not large enough to maintain in-house copy operations with dedicated tooling teams

**Decision Maker:**
- Founder
- Creative Director
- Head of Marketing
- Strategy Lead

**Geography:**
- English and Spanish-speaking markets
- Primary: United States, LATAM (Spanish-language agencies), EU English-speaking countries (UK, Ireland, Nordics)

## 2. Behavioral Profile

The ICP user:

- Runs 5 or more active copy projects per month across multiple clients
- Iterates on drafts 2–4 times before client delivery
- Needs structured comparison between draft versions, not one-shot generation
- Cares about quality signaling — needs something defensible to show clients when recommending a version
- Already uses AI in their workflow (ChatGPT, Claude, or similar) but does so without structure, version control, or scoring
- Has experienced version chaos: lost drafts, overwritten copy, disagreements with clients over "which version was better"
- Values a repeatable delivery process over creative spontaneity

---

## 3. Core Pain Points

1. **Version chaos** — Multiple draft iterations exist across Docs, Notion, or email threads. No single source of truth. No structured comparison between versions.

2. **Subjective decision-making** — Choosing the final version is based on opinion, client gut feel, or whoever has the loudest voice. There is no structured rationale to present.

3. **Manual iteration time** — Each round of refinement requires either rewriting manually or re-prompting a general-purpose AI tool with no memory of context or previous attempts.

4. **No quality signals** — Deliverables are approved on feel, not on criteria-aligned evaluation. Agencies cannot easily demonstrate why one version outperforms another.

5. **Difficult client justification** — When clients push back on a copy recommendation, there is no structured evidence to support the recommendation. Agencies absorb the revision cycle rather than defend the original.

---

## 4. Not Target Segment

The following segments are explicitly excluded from primary ICP definition:

| Segment | Reason Excluded |
|---|---|
| Hobby bloggers | Low output volume. No structured iteration need. Price sensitivity incompatible with agency-tier plans. |
| Solo casual creators with low output | No multi-version workflow need. General-purpose AI tools sufficient. |
| Enterprise marketing departments | Complex procurement, custom compliance requirements, team-scale needs not yet addressed. |
| Social-only micro-creators | Single-platform, single-format output. No comparison engine need. Speed > structure. |

**Enterprise Note:** Enterprise is not a current target, not a disqualified market. It is deferred pending team access and admin controls at scale.

## 5. Willingness to Pay Assumption

**[ASSUMPTION — Strategic Modeling Input Only]**

- Agencies treat productivity software as operational infrastructure, not a luxury spend.
- Monthly SaaS subscription is the preferred commercial model. Annual prepay may be acceptable for lower price tiers.
- ROI justification threshold: if CopyZap saves 3–5 hours of manual copy iteration per month per client engagement, at an average agency blended rate of $75–$150/hour, the payback period is immediate at any tier below $200/month.
- Assumed acceptable price range: **$49–$149/month per seat or per workspace**
- Price sensitivity increases significantly above $200/month without team-seat or enterprise feature differentiation.
